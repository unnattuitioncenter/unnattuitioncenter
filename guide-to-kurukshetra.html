Ancient History

1. Brahma Sarovar

Must Visit

Often described as the "Ocean of Purity", this vast water tank is the focal point of Kurukshetra. It is believed that Lord Brahma conceived the universe here.

  • Best Time to Visit: Sunrise or during the Evening Aarti (6:00 PM).
  • Significance: A dip here during a Solar Eclipse is considered equal to performing 1000 Ashwamedha Yajnas.
  • Tip for Students: Visit the North Bank to see the beautiful bronze chariot of Lord Krishna.

2. Jyotisar (Birthplace of Gita)

Located 5km from the main city, this is the exact spot where Lord Krishna stopped the chariot between the two armies to deliver the message of Karma Yoga to Arjuna.

Light & Sound Show: Don't miss the 3D projection mapping show in the evening. It brings the Mahabharata alive.

3. For Science & History Buffs

Sheikh Chehli's Tomb

A stunning example of Persian architecture. The marble dome and symmetrical gardens make it a photographer's paradise. It is protected by the ASI.

Kurukshetra Panorama & Science Centre

Highly Recommended for Students.

Ground floor: Interactive science exhibits (Physics, Math tricks).
Top floor: A lifelike 360-degree painting of the Mahabharata battle with war cries and sound effects.

Kalpana Chawla Planetarium

dedicated to the Haryana-born astronaut. The space theater shows are educational and inspiring for young minds aspiring for NASA/ISRO.
